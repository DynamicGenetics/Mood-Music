Study Information

We are currently looking for University of Bristol student participants!
If you are interested, then please make sure you have read the information on this page. You can click on the button at the bottom if you still want to be involved. If you have any questions you can email Nina.

We are doing a study to find out about how the music we listen to is related to our mood. To do this we are looking for people to fill out five surveys for us, every day for two weeks, and share their Spotify data with us, so we can use statistical methods to see if there is a relationship between the two.

The people responsible for this study are:
Nina Di Cara
Dr Oliver Davis
Prof Claire Haworth
We are a team at the MRC Integrative Epidemiology Unit at the University of Bristol.

Funding for the study is from the Jean Golding Institute, and the Elizabeth Blackwell Institute.

The study has been reviewed and approved by the Faculty of Health Sciences Research Ethics Committee (ID:101846). If you have any concerns about it you can contact research-governance@bristol.ac.uk.

You can take part if you are:
  • Over 18 years old
  • University of Bristol student
  • A Spotify Premium user who usually uses Spotify daily
There are a few things you will need to do, after you have consented to take part. All of them will be done online, or through your phone.
  1. Fill in some surveys about your wellbeing, mental health, personality traits, and tell us your age and gender. This won’t take longer than 20 minutes.
  2. Authorise the study to access your Spotify listening history though our website. This will prompt you to log into Spotify and approve access. We will use the authorisation code this generates to collect your listening data during the two-weeks you fill in daily surveys.
  3. Answer five questions about how you feel, five times a day for two weeks by text message.
  4. Lastly, we’ll ask you to download your listening data from Spotify (which covers the past year), and upload it to our website. We do this as well as Step 2 because your download contains songs you have skipped, as well as the songs you have listened to and also means we can see whether people’s listening habits change when they are taking part in a study.
Anyone who completes all four stages will get a £30 shopping voucher.
If you are a Psychology student, you will be able to claim 3 hours towards the Experimental Hours scheme. As a ‘THANK YOU!’ for taking part, you will also get a visualisation of you Spotify data.
Benefits You will be contributing to a brand-new study and helping us to understand the ways in which our wellbeing affects our behaviour.
This research is working towards helping us understand mental health, wellbeing and behaviour better.
Disadvantages/Risks The study will ask you questions about how you feel regularly throughout the day, for 2 weeks. You should think about whether you will be comfortable to reflect on how you are feeling this often. At the beginning there is also a questionnaire which asks questions about your wellbeing.

We will be taking several steps to keep your data safe, and make sure your confidentiality is maintained. All data will be stored and managed in line with GDPR requirements.

  • All data we collect will be stored securely, in a location approved for research data storage.
  • Data that might reveal who you are, such as your email address, will be kept securely and separately to all the other data about you, and will only be accessible by the study team.
  • For all the other data we collect, we will store it against a participant number, instead of your name, to maintain your confidentiality.
  • At the end of the study we will make your listening history (the list of songs you have listened to, and the date/time your listened to them), and your survey data (your age, gender, initial surveys and daily wellbeing surveys) available as an anonymous dataset, so you will not be identifiable. Anyone who wants to use the dataset will have to ask to get access to it. This data will be stored by the University’s Research Data Storage Facility.
  • Within 60 days of the data collection finishing, we will securely destroy your identifiable information, such as your email address. At this point we will no longer know whose data is whose.
  • The only reason that we would break our promise of confidentiality during the study is if we were so concerned about your mental health that we thought you might be a risk to yourself, or to somebody else. In this situation we would call the emergency services.
You can withdraw from the study at any time by emailing Nina (nina.dicara@bristol.ac.uk). You do not need to have a reason, just let us know.
If it is before everyone’s identifiable data has been deleted, we can delete all your data from the study. If it is afterwards then we can’t delete your data, because we will not know which it is.

When we have done all of our analysis, we will write a blog on the Jean Golding Institute’s website. After that, we will write an academic research article about the results, so that other people can find out about what we have done. The data we share in these articles will never contain identifying information.

So that you can stay informed, we will post links to these articles on the study website. That way, you can check back and read about the results.

We'd be happy to answer any questions you have! Email Nina at nina.dicara@bristol.ac.uk.
